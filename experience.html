Research Assistant, SAIL

To date this is the position I grew the most in, I had a supportive lab and worked for an amazing Professor. Although it was in my toughest year of undergrad, it set the standard of work environments very high. The learning curve was steep, with the main goal to:

Improve existing MATLAB code by conducting tests on gearing/stepper ratios of motors, varying acceleration and speed limits using the tuff-tilt inclinometer to ensure proper calibration of software for sidereal coordinate system star tracking of an equatorial telescope mount.

I literally started not knowing what sidereal tracking even meant.

Being given a chance to learn while working, with a team that prioritizes understanding in an environment that was so warm was a blessing, deserves a separate blogpost to be fair. Dr. Enright turned what should’ve been a huge failure to my greatest lesson. So I was tasked with testing the mount multiple times to ensure the code was responding correctly to commands, and the tuft-tilt inclinometer was the main measuring tool. Ahn. I literally tried pulling an all-nighter after figuring out how to automate the test process, not really paying attention to the results the inclinometer was giving. Time to share my findings and we notice there were slight differences in the orientation command and the inclinometer readings. Then as the researcher the Prof is, my next task was to find out the source of the error. Honestly the PhD student needed the inclinometer for something else and was so confused as to why the task was taking so long since the error was from my not giving some time between each command. Okay so that’s 2 lessons, because why didn’t I just ask for help from the expert or brainstorm at the very least? The main lesson was in understanding how to create test plans, tools being used and the value of sanity tests during the process as opposed to after hours of work.

This experience also served as a wake up call, “DO NOT GHOST YOURSELF” is what I have at the back of my mind when I sense I am about to give up of stop trying. Something that started off as doing too much in the heaviest semester, turned into complacency, then the lie of “I’m too behind” then ghosting. Really all I needed to do was communicate the situation, and if I wasn’t working for such a patient/empathetic it would have been a different story. Ghosting is not an option, so be conscious of how much you can take on.
